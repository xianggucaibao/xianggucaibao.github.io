CITES Wildlife Trade Database¶

The Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora CITES is an international treaty organisation tasked with monitoring, reporting and providing recommendations on the international species trade. It is one of the oldest conservation organisations in existence.

Participation is voluntary, but almost every member nation in the UN (and therefore almost every country worldwide) participates. Countries participating in CITES are obligated to report on roughly 5000 animal species and 29000 plant species brought into or exported out of their countries, and to honor limitations placed on the international trade of these species.

Protected species are organised into three appendixes:

  • Appendix I species are those whose trade threatens them with extinction. Two particularly famous examples of Class I species are the black rhinoceros and the African elephant, whose extremely valuable tusks are an alluring target for poachers exporting ivory abroad.
  • Appendix II species are those not threatened with extinction, but whose trade is nevertheless detrimental. Most species in CITES are in Appendix II.
  • Appendix III animals are those submitted to CITES by member states as a control mechanism. Their export or import requires permits from the submitting member state(s).

The CITES records are publicly available via the CITES Trade Database website. The dataset we will be using for our analysis record all legal species imports and exports carried out in 2019 (wildlife_trade_2019) that were reported to CITES. Species not on the CITES lists and illegal black market trades are not included.

The dataset contains columns identifying the species, the import and export countries, and the amount and characteristics of the goods being traded (which range from live animals to skins and cadavers). A detailed description of each of the fields can be found in the original CITES documentation.

key_words = ["crocodylus", "alligator", "caiman"]
counter = 0
species_list = []

for _, row in trade_2019.iterrows():
    for word in key_words:
        if word in row["Taxon"]:
            counter += 1
            
            species_name = row["Taxon"]
            
            if species_name not in species_list:
                species_list.append(species_name)
        
print("Number of crocodile / alligator / caiman type species traded: ", counter)
print(species_list)
